Instructions

Vision span or perceptual span refers to the angular span (vertically and horizontally), within which the human eye has sharp enough vision to perform an action, such as reading, accurately.

In the following tasks, you will be asked to focus on a red dot while attempting to read a series of trigrams, i.e., three letters, in the periphery. Try not to move your eyes away from where the red dot was!

  1. First, please fix your gaze on the red dot in the center of the screen.
  2. Once the dot disappears, a quick flash of three letters will appear on the screen. After each flash, you have five seconds to read out loud the letters you just saw from left to right.
  3. Then, the red dot will reappear to help you refocus your eyes on the correct spot, after which the next trigram will be shown
